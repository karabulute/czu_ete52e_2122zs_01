  • Grandhotel Pupp - the history of the hotel dates back to the beginning of the 18th century, when Anton Deiml built a lusthaus called the Saxon Hall at a relatively large distance from the city center (the construction was financed by the Saxon Elector August II). A few years later, the city had a so-called Czech Hall built in the neighborhood . In the second half of the 18th century, the confectioner Johann Georg Pupp bought the Czech Hall, but his grandchildren only managed to unite the Czech and Saxon Halls. In the 1990s, it was rebuilt into a hotel complex under the leadership of Fellner and Helmer.
  • Hotel Beseda - today a residential house with a historical connection to the beginnings of the Czech community in Karlovy Vary, which founded the Slovanská beseda association in the second half of the 19th century . Following the association, the Beseda house with hotel rooms, a restaurant and a beer hall was built at the beginning of the 20th century. After the signing of the Munich Agreement, the association's activities were terminated and the hotel was converted into apartments for the Reich Railways. The activities of the association have not been resumed.
  • Hotel Imperial was built in 1912 for a wealthy spa clientele. A 127-meter-long cable car from Theater Square leads to the Imperial Heights.
  • Hotel Palacky is located on the left bank of the river Teplá directly in the spa center. It is one of the oldest buildings in the area, which was built in the mid-18th century.
  • Grandhotel Ambassador - before reconstruction name National House - Art Nouveau hotel with a pseudo pseudo elements, designed by a Viennese studio of architects Fellner and Helmer in 1899-1900, was built in less than year 1901. The construction was initiated Carlsbad ck Rifle Corps , hence the original name sounded Grandhotel The shooting house , after being renamed the Ambassador, operated the Orpheus variety show and four restaurants. In 1930, the hotel became the property of the city and after that it became known as the National House (Volkshaus). In the 1990s, the hotel underwent a complicated privatization, which continued until 2010 in a lawsuit between the new owners and the city. Extensive reconstruction took place in 2011–2015. [21]Now it serves the original purposes again. It includes the Julius Zeyer restaurant , Nároďák , a café and a social hall (originally the Orpheus variety show). The frescoes on the facades depict scenes from the city's military history, made by Viennese painters Max Kurzweil and Wilhelm List.
  • Parkhotel Richmond - the original mill was first rebuilt at the beginning of the 19th century into the Schönbrun café, which continued to expand until it became a hotel at the turn of the 19th and 20th centuries. The building has since undergone several reconstructions, the last of which took place between 1998 and 2005.
  • Hotel Atlantic Palace - formerly the Atlantic Spa House, an Art Nouveau spa building built in the early 20th century in the Art Deco style.
  • Poštovní dvůr - originally a post office with a switching station for postal horse-drawn carriages, built in 1781 in the Classicist style, then rebuilt in the Empire style in 1810.

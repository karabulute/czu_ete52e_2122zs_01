  • Church of St. Mary Magdalene is a single-nave Baroque building on Kostelní náměstí from 1732–1736, built by Kilián Ignác Dientzenhofer on the ground plan of an ellipse with niches for chapels, with distinctly sculpted cornices and a chancel. A two-armed staircase and two towers with onion-shaped domes protrude in the façade. The ceiling paintings and the altarpiece were painted by Elijah Dollhopf , the main altarpiece by JJ Mady depicts Christ at the apparition of Mary Magdalene , two Gothic statues of the Madonna, four statues on the main altar were carved by Jakub Eberle and represent St. Augustine, St. Paul, St. Jerome and St. Petra; there is a crypt under the floor.
  • Orthodox Church of St. Peter and Paul in the years 1893–1897 designed by architect Gustav Wiedermann , class King George, is a five-dome building modeled on the Byzantine-Old Russian church in Ostankino near Moscow. It was built with the help of patrons from the Russian aristocracy. The relief of the sculptor M. Hiller depicts Tsar Peter I during the construction of the U páva house in 1711.
  • Church of St. Urbana (13th century) Rybáře, Horní Kamenná - rebuilt after 1500 using the original elements of an older church or chapel, it is the oldest preserved church building in the city, it was probably built in the second half of the 13th century as part of colonization.
  • Church of St. Peter and Paul from 1856 , architect J. Zeissig, choir dr. Karel Farský , until 1946 Evangelical Church of St. Petra, Mariánskolázeňská ul. Built in neo-Romanesque style. In 1864, a tower was added and construction of the neighboring rectory began. The interior of the church forms an interesting altarpiece of the Ascension of Christ. A wooden carved pulpit stands on three columns. In the nave of the church there are temperate wooden pews and a metal baptismal font. The organ on the bar comes from organist Müller from Cheb. On the walls in the hall there is also a younger relief of Petr Chelčický by Franta Anýž and a relief bust of ThDr. Karel Farský .
  • Pilgrimage Church of St. Anny in Sedlec built in the years 1739–1748, architect V. Schmidt. It is located in a village with a history dating back to the 11th century. Baroque altar, ancient statue of St. Anny with Maria and the baby.
  • Greek Catholic Cemetery Church of St. Ondřej from the 16th century, Ondřejská Street, the oldest church in Karlovy Vary, originally Gothic, renovated several times. The adjacent cemetery was abolished in 1911, changed to Mozart's orchards .
  • Anglican Church of St. Lukáš from 1877 , architect dr. Mothes, on Castle Hill. Built in neo-Gothic style with the financial support of patrons from the ranks of English spa guests.
  • Church of the Ascension in Stará Rola from 1909, on the site of an older chapel from 1823.
  • Church of the Exaltation of St. Kříže in Rybářy near the church of St. Urbana.

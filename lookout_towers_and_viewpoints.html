  • Mayer's Gloriet, also sometimes called the Deer Leap Viewpoint, is a resting place with a gazebo overlooking the city.
  • The statue of Kamzík , called Deer Leap, was created in 1851 by the sculptor August Kiss at the request of Baron Lützow , who wanted to ridicule the legend of the founding of Karlovy Vary.
  • Otto's column on Otto's height refers in its name to the first Greek king who visited Karlovy Vary a total of five times during the 19th century. The granite column topped with a sphere and a gilded cross, located near the lookout, was erected in 1852 in the presence of King Otto I himself. In 2008, the column was reconstructed. The viewpoint offers a view of the city center.

  • The Imperial Spa (Spa I) was built at the end of the 19th century according to the architects Ferdinand Fellner and Hermann Helmer . There were 120 bathrooms and cabins in the Císařské lázně building, as well as an imperial bath. Zander's Hall, which originally served as a gymnasium, was transformed into a casino at the turn of the 1980s and 1990s. In 2010, the spa building became a national cultural monument.
  • The Vřídelní Spa (Lázně II) was located near the Vřídlo and offered hot and carbonic baths. In 1940, the spa was abolished and in 1947 demolished.
  • The Kurhaus (Spa III) was built between 1864 and 1866 and still serves its purpose today. Originally, the building had a steam bath with a swimming pool and baths for carbonic baths and peat wraps.
  • Nové Lázně (Lázně IV) currently serves as a shopping center. In the past, however, the town hall was also located here. The building was built in the late 19th century.
  • Elizabeth's Spa (Spa V) is named after Empress Elisabeth of Bavaria (Sissi) . The building was built in the early 20th century. During the reconstruction at the turn of the 1960s and 1970s, the spa became the largest spa in Czechoslovakia. During the reconstruction, the spa was also supplemented with a swimming pool. Another reconstruction took place in 2002.
  • Sluneční lázně (Lázně VI) is an unpreserved monument. The spa was the youngest spa in Karlovy Vary built in 1927. The building was demolished in 2006 for static reasons. The Lázně VI Apartment Building was built on the site of the building.
